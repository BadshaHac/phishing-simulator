How to Identify Phishing Attempts

üîç Check the Sender

Suspicious Email Addresses

Look for misspellings, extra characters, or unusual domains:

  • ‚ùå support@goog1e.com (number 1 instead of l)
  • ‚ùå noreply@amazon-security.net (unofficial domain)
  • ‚úÖ support@google.com (official domain)

Generic Greetings

Legitimate companies use your name:

  • ‚ùå "Dear Customer"
  • ‚ùå "Dear User"
  • ‚úÖ "Dear John Smith"

‚ö†Ô∏è Warning Signs in Content

Urgency and Threats

Phishing emails create panic:

  • "Act within 24 hours or lose access"
  • "Your account will be deleted"
  • "Immediate action required"

Too Good to Be True

Be skeptical of:

  • Unexpected prizes or rewards
  • Huge discounts (90% off)
  • Free money or gifts

üîó Suspicious Links

Hover Before Clicking

Always check where links actually go:

Displayed: Click here to verify account

Actual: http://malicious-site.com/steal-info

URL Red Flags

  • Misspelled domains (amazom.com)
  • Extra subdomains (paypal.fake-site.com)
  • URL shorteners hiding destination
  • HTTP instead of HTTPS on login pages

üìù Poor Quality

Grammar and Spelling

Professional companies proofread:

  • Multiple spelling errors
  • Poor grammar
  • Awkward phrasing
  • Inconsistent formatting

Unprofessional Design

  • Blurry logos or images
  • Misaligned text
  • Wrong colors or fonts
  • Missing company branding

üõ°Ô∏è How to Protect Yourself

1. Verify Independently

Don't click email links. Instead, go directly to the company's website by typing the URL yourself.

2. Check with the Company

Call the company using a phone number from their official website, not from the suspicious email.

3. Use Two-Factor Authentication

Enable 2FA on all important accounts to add an extra layer of security.

4. Keep Software Updated

Update your browser, operating system, and security software regularly.

5. Report Phishing

Forward phishing emails to the Anti-Phishing Working Group at reportphishing@apwg.org

6. Use Spam Filters

Enable and maintain spam filters in your email client to catch many phishing attempts.
